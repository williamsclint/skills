# FAQ Guide: Org Change Memos

Use this guide to generate the FAQ section of a change memo. Anticipate the questions people will actually ask — including the uncomfortable ones.

## Category 1: Job Security
- Will my job be affected?
- Are there more changes coming?
- Is this the beginning of layoffs?
- Should I be worried about my role?

*Guidance: Answer directly. If roles are safe, say so clearly. If uncertain, say what you know and when more will be decided. Never imply safety if it isn't true.*

## Category 2: Reporting & Structure
- Who do I report to now?
- Who is my skip-level?
- Will my team change?
- What happens to my current manager?

*Guidance: Be specific. Name names where appropriate. If reporting lines are still being finalized, say so and give a date by which it will be resolved.*

## Category 3: Compensation & Benefits
- Does this affect my pay?
- Will my bonus or equity be affected?
- Do my benefits change?

*Guidance: If compensation is unchanged, say so explicitly — it reduces anxiety significantly. If there are changes, describe them clearly.*

## Category 4: Day-to-Day Work
- Do I need to do anything differently starting [date]?
- Will my responsibilities change?
- Will I be working with different people?
- Does my location or schedule change?

*Guidance: Focus on practical impact. People want to know what Monday morning looks like.*

## Category 5: Process & Systems
- Will the tools I use change?
- Will workflows or approvals change?
- Who do I go to for [X] now?

*Guidance: Be specific about what changes and what stays the same operationally.*

## Category 6: Rationale & Trust
- Why is this happening now?
- Was this planned or reactive?
- Did leadership know about this?
- How long has this been in the works?

*Guidance: Answer honestly. If this was planned, say so. If it was reactive to circumstances, explain the circumstances. Perceived deception damages trust more than the news itself.*

## Category 7: People Affected
- What happened to [person who left or changed roles]?
- Will [person] be okay?
- Can I reach out to [departed person]?

*Guidance: Be respectful of individuals' privacy while being clear about the change. If someone left voluntarily vs. involuntarily matters — handle accordingly with HR guidance.*

## Category 8: What Comes Next
- When will we hear more?
- Will there be a meeting to discuss this?
- Who can I talk to if I have questions?
- When will the transition be complete?

*Guidance: Give a concrete next touchpoint — a meeting date, a follow-up memo date, or a named contact. "We'll share more as we know it" without a date is frustrating.*

---

## FAQ Drafting Tips

- Include 5–8 questions maximum in the memo itself
- Rank by what people will most want to know, not what's easiest to answer
- Write questions in first-person ("Will my job be affected?") not third-person ("How will this affect employees?")
- If the answer is "we don't know yet," say when you will know
- Never answer a question with a non-answer — if you can't answer it, explain why
