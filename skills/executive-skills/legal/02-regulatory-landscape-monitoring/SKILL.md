# Regulatory Landscape Monitoring

**Persona:** Head of Legal / CLO
**Classification:** INTERNAL USE ONLY
**Department:** Office of the General Counsel | Regulatory Affairs

## Description
This skill enables the CLO to maintain a real-time, comprehensive view of Wynn Resorts' regulatory obligations across all operating jurisdictions, including gaming commissions, securities regulators, AML authorities, and data protection regulators. It synthesizes the jurisdiction register, the regulatory change log, and the compliance calendar to identify upcoming obligations, assess the business impact of regulatory changes, and ensure no filing or examination deadline is missed. The skill is the primary tool for managing Wynn's complex multi-jurisdiction licensing and compliance posture.

## When to Use
- When a new regulation, statute, or regulatory guidance has been issued in any jurisdiction where Wynn operates
- When quarterly compliance calendar review is being conducted and upcoming obligations must be confirmed
- When a gaming commission examination is scheduled and preparation activities must be coordinated
- When the CLO must brief the CEO, CFO, or board on the current regulatory risk landscape
- When a new jurisdiction or business activity is being evaluated and the licensing and compliance implications must be scoped

## Input Files Expected
| # | Document Name | Purpose |
|---|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Jurisdiction Inventory and Regulatory Body Register | Maps all operating jurisdictions, license types, expiry dates, and relationship owners to provide the compliance universe |
| 2 | Regulatory Change Monitoring Log (Q1 2025) | Tracks recent regulatory changes, their effective dates, business impact assessments, and required actions |
| 3 | Regulatory and Compliance Obligations Calendar (FY2025) | Provides the complete filing and reporting obligation calendar across all jurisdictions with due dates and owners |

## Output
A regulatory monitoring briefing including: a 90-day forward-looking obligations calendar with at-risk items highlighted, an impact assessment for all regulatory changes logged in the quarter, a license status summary with upcoming renewal deadlines, and a recommended action list prioritized by risk and deadline. For board presentations, includes a one-page regulatory risk landscape summary.

## Example Prompt
> "Give me a regulatory briefing for the next 90 days. What filings are due, what regulatory changes from Q1 do we need to act on, and are any of our licenses up for renewal? Flag anything that requires CEO or board attention."
