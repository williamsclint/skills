# Risk Quantification

**Persona:** CFO
**Classification:** STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL
**Department:** Office of the CFO | Enterprise Risk Management

## Description
This skill enables the CFO to assess, size, and prioritize financial and enterprise risks using structured quantitative methods. It synthesizes the enterprise risk register, insurance program, and scenario/sensitivity models to produce a financially grounded view of the company's risk exposure, coverage gaps, and tail scenarios. The output is designed for use by the Chief Risk Officer, Audit Committee, and CFO in risk reporting and mitigation planning.

## When to Use
- When the quarterly risk register needs to be reviewed and financial impact estimates updated
- When a new macro or operational risk event emerges and rapid quantification is required
- When the annual insurance renewal is approaching and coverage adequacy must be assessed
- When the board or rating agencies request a formal scenario analysis with downside quantification
- When a capital allocation or M&A decision requires an integrated risk-adjusted return view

## Input Files Expected
| # | Document Name | Purpose |
|---|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Enterprise Risk Register (Q1 2025) | Provides the current inventory of enterprise risks with likelihood, impact, and risk score ratings |
| 2 | Insurance Coverage Schedule (FY2025) | Maps insurance limits and deductibles to risk categories to identify coverage gaps against the risk register |
| 3 | FY2025 Scenario Planning & Sensitivity Analysis | Quantifies revenue and EBITDA impact across bear/base/bull scenarios to size financial exposure |

## Output
A risk quantification summary including: a ranked financial exposure table by risk category, an insurance coverage gap analysis mapping uninsured tail exposure, a scenario-weighted expected loss calculation, and prioritized mitigation recommendations with cost-benefit framing. Designed for direct insertion into a board risk committee or Audit Committee pack.

## Example Prompt
> "Map our top enterprise risks against our insurance coverage and tell me where we have uninsured exposure above $10M. Then show me what the bear case scenario implies for our debt coverage ratio and whether it triggers any covenant thresholds."
