# Executive Communication & Financial Storytelling

**Persona:** CFO
**Classification:** STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL
**Department:** Office of the CFO

## Description
This skill transforms complex financial data into clear, credible, and audience-appropriate narratives for boards, investors, and internal stakeholders. It draws on the CFO's communication style guide, approved messaging, and actual performance data to draft board packs, investor Q&A responses, and all-hands talking points that are accurate, on-message, and legally reviewed. The skill enforces discipline around approved terminology and sensitivity classifications.

## When to Use
- When drafting the CFO section of a board finance committee pack or board deck
- When preparing for an earnings call and investor Q&A rehearsal is required
- When all-hands or town hall financial commentary needs to be crafted for employee audiences
- When a sensitive financial topic (guidance, impairment, restructuring) must be communicated with precision
- When a junior team member's draft narrative needs to be reviewed against CFO communication standards

## Input Files Expected
| # | Document Name | Purpose |
|---|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Board Finance Committee Pack (Q4 2024) | Provides the current-period financial narrative baseline and the structure/tone used for board-level communication |
| 2 | Investor Q&A Response Log (FY2024 Earnings Cycle) | Supplies approved, legally-cleared language for sensitive topics raised by the investor community |
| 3 | CFO Communication Standards and Style Guide | Defines approved terminology, messaging hierarchy by audience, and items explicitly to avoid |

## Output
Audience-appropriate draft communication outputs including: a structured CFO board narrative (with data references), draft investor Q&A responses for anticipated questions, talking points for internal audiences, and a red-line review of any draft submitted for compliance with communication standards. Flagged items include unapproved terminology and statements requiring legal clearance.

## Example Prompt
> "Draft my opening remarks for the Board Finance Committee meeting covering Q4 2024 performance. Use the board pack as the data source but make sure the narrative follows our communication standards and avoids any language flagged as sensitive in the investor Q&A log."
