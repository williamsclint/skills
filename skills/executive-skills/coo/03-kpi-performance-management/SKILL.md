# KPI Definition & Performance Management

**Persona:** COO
**Classification:** INTERNAL USE ONLY
**Department:** Office of the COO | Operations Analytics

## Description
This skill enables the COO to design, maintain, and act on a rigorous performance management system across all operational departments. It synthesizes the executive scorecard, industry benchmark data, and lessons-learned from prior KPI reviews to assess whether current metrics are the right ones, whether targets are appropriately calibrated, and where performance requires intervention. The skill supports both the monthly operating review cadence and the annual KPI framework redesign.

## When to Use
- When preparing for the monthly or quarterly operational performance review with department VPs
- When benchmark data reveals that Wynn's performance on a specific metric lags luxury peers significantly
- When the annual KPI framework review is underway and decisions must be made about which metrics to retire, add, or reweight
- When a department VP disputes a KPI target as unrealistic and the COO needs a data-grounded response
- When a RAG status on the executive scorecard triggers an escalation and a structured response plan is required

## Input Files Expected
| # | Document Name | Purpose |
|---|---------------|---------|
| 1 | COO Executive Scorecard — February 2025 | Provides the current-month KPI performance across all operational, guest experience, financial, and people dimensions |
| 2 | Hospitality Operations Benchmark Report — 2024 | Supplies external benchmarks from luxury hospitality peers and industry databases (STR, HVS) to contextualize internal performance |
| 3 | Annual KPI Framework Review — FY2024 Lessons Learned | Documents which KPIs worked, which were not useful, and what changes were recommended for the current year |

## Output
A performance management briefing including: a scorecard summary with RAG status analysis and trend narrative, benchmark gap analysis highlighting where Wynn over- or underperforms relative to luxury peers, a prioritized list of underperforming KPIs with root cause hypotheses and intervention options, and recommendations for KPI additions, retirements, or target resets. For formal reviews, includes a draft VP accountability memo.

## Example Prompt
> "I have my monthly ops review tomorrow. Pull the February scorecard, flag everything that's red or amber, and tell me for each underperforming metric whether we're also behind our luxury peer benchmark or just behind our internal target. Prioritize the top three issues I should address."
