# Cross-Functional Coordination

**Persona:** COO
**Classification:** INTERNAL USE ONLY
**Department:** Office of the COO | Strategy & Operations

## Description
This skill enables the COO to align multi-department initiatives, resolve ownership ambiguity, and drive accountability across Wynn Resorts' complex organizational structure. It synthesizes OKR frameworks, RACI matrices, and interdepartmental service agreements to identify coordination gaps, misaligned incentives, and accountability failures before they become execution problems. The skill is particularly valuable for high-visibility cross-functional projects where failure to coordinate creates guest-facing or financial risk.

## When to Use
- When a major cross-departmental project (e.g., new outlet opening, technology rollout) requires clear ownership mapping
- When an initiative is stalling and it is unclear which department is the bottleneck or decision-maker
- When OKR alignment across departments is being established or reviewed at the start of a quarter
- When an SLA between two departments is consistently being missed and escalation is required
- When the COO needs to conduct a cross-functional readiness review ahead of a major launch or event

## Input Files Expected
| # | Document Name | Purpose |
|---|---------------|---------|
| 1 | OKR Framework & Current Objectives (Q1 2025) | Provides the organizational objectives and key results to assess whether cross-functional work is aligned to strategic priorities |
| 2 | RACI Matrix — New F&B Outlet Opening | Defines responsibility assignments across all departments for a live project to identify ownership gaps and conflicts |
| 3 | Interdepartmental SLA Agreement — IT Services to Operations | Supplies the formal service commitments and escalation paths between two critical departments as a model and reference |

## Output
A cross-functional coordination assessment including: an ownership gap analysis against the RACI, OKR alignment check for all departments involved, SLA compliance status for relevant service agreements, a prioritized list of coordination risks with recommended interventions, and a draft escalation memo if a critical dependency is at risk. For launch-readiness reviews, includes a go/no-go recommendation with conditions.

## Example Prompt
> "The Lakeside Kitchen launch is six weeks out. Review the RACI, check whether all departments have their OKRs aligned to the launch, and identify any SLA obligations between IT and Operations that could create a readiness risk. Give me a launch readiness summary I can present at the executive team meeting."
