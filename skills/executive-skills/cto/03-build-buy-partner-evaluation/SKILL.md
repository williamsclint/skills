# Build vs Buy vs Partner Evaluation

**Persona:** CTO / Head of IT
**Classification:** STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL
**Department:** Office of the CTO | Technology Strategy

## Description
This skill enables the CTO to conduct disciplined, data-grounded evaluations of whether to build proprietary technology, purchase commercial software, or establish a technology partnership for a given capability need. It synthesizes technology business cases, historical build/buy decision outcomes, and vendor market landscape analyses to produce an objective recommendation with financial modeling, risk assessment, and a documented decision rationale. The skill counteracts common biases such as over-optimism in build estimates and under-estimation of vendor lock-in risk.

## When to Use
- When a significant technology investment decision (greater than $1M) is pending and a formal business case must be prepared
- When a vendor has proposed a partnership or licensing arrangement that requires evaluation against internal build capability
- When reviewing historical build/buy decisions to improve future decision quality
- When a vendor RFI or RFP process is underway and shortlisting criteria must be established
- When the board technology committee requires a structured technology investment recommendation

## Input Files Expected
| # | Document Name | Purpose |
|---|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Technology Business Case - Next-Generation Loyalty Platform | Provides the specific capability need, financial model, and initial build/buy/partner analysis for evaluation |
| 2 | Build vs Buy Decision Outcomes - Historical Review (FY2020-2024) | Supplies base rates for budget accuracy, on-time delivery, and ROI achievement across prior decisions to calibrate current projections |
| 3 | Vendor Market Landscape Analysis - Customer Data Platform | Demonstrates the vendor evaluation methodology and market landscape to contextualize the options available |

## Output
A build/buy/partner decision recommendation including: structured options analysis with pros/cons/cost/timeline/risk for each path, a historical base-rate calibration adjusting projections based on prior outcomes, a vendor scorecard if commercial options are in scope, a 5-year TCO comparison across options, and a final recommendation with rationale formatted as an Architecture Decision Record. Includes a one-page executive summary for board or investment committee submission.

## Example Prompt
> "We need to decide whether to build our next-generation loyalty platform in-house, buy a commercial solution, or partner with a vendor. Use the business case, our historical track record on build projects, and the CDP vendor landscape analysis to give me a recommendation I can present to the board technology committee."
